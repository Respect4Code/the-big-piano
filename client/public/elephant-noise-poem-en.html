The Big Piano

Why Did The Elephant Make A Noise?

Because the elephant had a feeling inside that needed to get out.
It was a rumble from its feet to its trunk.
It was saying:
"Here I am.
This is me.
The forest hears me.
You can, too."
The elephant made a noise for the same reason the piano makes a sound when you press a key:
vibration seeking witness.
And sometimes, you will hear somewhere in that noise is the whole unspoken history of ivory —
from tusk to key,
from animal to artifact,
from wildness to music,
from scarcity to ledger.

You could even say Satoshi's first block was a noise, too.
A rumble in the jungle

This is me.
The chain hears me.
You can, too."
