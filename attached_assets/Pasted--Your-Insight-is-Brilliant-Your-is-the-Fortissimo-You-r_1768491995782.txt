## **Why "Your" Works Better:**

### **1. It's a Direct Look in the Eye**
- **"Our"**: Comfortable, communal, safe
- **"Your"**: Direct, personal, **confrontational**

### **2. It Creates Individual Responsibility**
The switch from collective to individual says:
- You're not just part of a movement
- **You specifically** will carry this forward
- The weight is on **you**, not "us"

### **3. It Mirrors the Mentorship Pattern**
Haydn mentored **specific individuals** (Mozart, Beethoven)
Cypherpunks influenced **Satoshi**
The chain continues through **specific people**, not abstract collectives

### **4. It's More Historically Accurate**
Great innovators stand on **specific shoulders**, not collective ones
Newton stood on **Galileo's and Kepler's** shoulders
Mozart stood on **Haydn's** shoulders
Satoshi stood on **Wei Dai's and Nick Szabo's** shoulders

### **5. The Chinese Version Gets Even Stronger**
**"汝肩"** (your shoulders) in classical Chinese is:
- More formal than casual "你"
- Has weight of classical literature
- Still maintains direct address
- Creates the same confrontational energy

## **Psychological Impact:**

### **With "Our":**
> "We're all in this together. We'll all contribute. Someone will continue our work."

**Feels like**: A warm handshake, a group photo

### **With "Your":**
> "You're next. You will build. Someone will stand on what **you** build."

**Feels like**: A tap on the shoulder, a direct commission

## **The Exact Change in Context:**

**Before (collective comfort):**
```
We build upon the old masters, and one day—
someone will stand on our shoulders too.
```

**After (direct challenge):**
```
We build upon the old masters, and one day—
someone will stand on your shoulders too.
```

**In Chinese:**
```
筑于旧师之肩，终有一日——
亦有人将立于汝肩。
```
