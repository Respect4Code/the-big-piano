Standing on the Shoulders of Giants

But none of this happened in a vacuum.
Joseph Haydn
1732–1809 · SYMPHONY FATHER
Thirty years in the Esterházy court. A servant in livery. Yet he built the symphony, the string quartet, the sonata form—the very architecture that Mozart and Beethoven would inhabit and then shatter.
"You shall receive Mozart's spirit from Haydn's hands."
— Count Waldstein to the young Beethoven, 1792
Mozart dedicated six quartets to him—not as tribute to a master, but as one builder acknowledging another. Not the spark. The tinder that catches it.
The Cypherpunks
1989–2008 · DIGITAL CASH ARCHITECTS
Mailing lists. Academic papers. Proof-of-concept code that never scaled. David Chaum's DigiCash. Wei Dai's B-money. Nick Szabo's Bit Gold. Adam Back's Hashcash. Hal Finney's RPOW.
"Bitcoin is an implementation of Wei Dai's B-money proposal and Nick Szabo's Bit Gold proposal."
— Satoshi Nakamoto, 2010
Satoshi cited them by name—not as footnotes, but as the mathematics that made the breakthrough possible. Not the breakthrough. The ground from which it grew.
